Our Story

Compared to the cortex, our understanding of the white matter motor tracts that run beneath it is still in its early stages. Cadaveric and imaging studies have shown that the corticospinal tract (CST) descends from the precentral gyrus through the corona radiata into the internal capsule, condensing into a swirl-like configuration as its fibres twist and rotate — the most medial cortical fibres becoming posterior at the internal capsule and lateral at the cerebral peduncle. Primate research has further shown how this fibre organization corresponds to functional body areas in dynamic, complex ways.

Understanding this anatomy in detail is essential for safe surgery near subcortical motor pathways. Diffusion tensor imaging (DTI) has become a valuable starting point, with DTI-based subcortical maps now common tools for pre-operative planning — yet image-based techniques alone still fall short of the accuracy needed for confident, safe surgery, which is why intraoperative direct stimulation of subcortical motor pathways remains the gold standard.

NeuroTraxx grew out of this gap — work exploring how ultra-high-field structural and diffusion MRI could be turned into living, interactive 3D maps of the brain to better bridge pre-operative imaging and intraoperative navigation, built to support subcortical navigation, MER, and DBS workflows. What started as a research tool is now being developed into a focused desktop application for researchers and clinicians.

Vision

The map of the brain is still being drawn. Even today, researchers are uncovering new details about how the brain is wired — and every new discovery is a chance to navigate, treat, and understand it better.

We're building NeuroTraxx for that future: a tool that grows alongside the science, making the brain's wiring easier to see, explore, and act on — for the researchers and clinicians pushing the field forward today, and for whatever comes next.
