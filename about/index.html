Our Story

NeuroTraxx grew out of work exploring how ultra-high-field structural and diffusion MRI could be turned into living, interactive 3D maps of the brain — built to support subcortical navigation, MER, and DBS workflows. What started as a research tool is now being developed into a focused desktop application for researchers and clinicians.

Vision

Our understanding of subcortical anatomy is still evolving. Recent intraoperative mapping research — based on data from 180 patients undergoing brain tumor surgery — proposed a "supplemental cortical motor homunculus," adding the previously undocumented representation of intercostal muscles to Penfield's classical 1937 motor map, and introduced a novel subcortical map showing how motor fibers reorganize as they descend from cortex through white matter tracts (Wang et al., 2021). This was the first major update to motor mapping principles in nearly a century, and it underscores how much remains to be refined about the brain's wiring.

NeuroTraxx is built on the belief that as anatomical understanding advances, the tools researchers and clinicians use to navigate the brain should keep pace — giving them fast, interactive, synchronized 2D/3D views of structural and diffusion imaging so new findings can be explored, validated, and applied at the bedside and in the lab.
